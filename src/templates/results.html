Learning Outcomes

Key Strategic Concepts Applied
Porter's Five Forces

This simulation demonstrated how competitive forces shape strategy in the smartphone industry:

  • Competitive Rivalry: Teams directly competed for market share across different segments
  • Supplier Power: Component quality and costs affected product development
  • Buyer Power: Different market segments showed varying price sensitivity
  • Threat of New Entrants: Strategic events introduced new competitors
  • Threat of Substitutes: Innovation was required to maintain relevance
Generic Strategies

Teams employed different strategic approaches:

  • Cost Leadership: Competing on price in budget segments
  • Differentiation: Creating unique value through quality and features
  • Focus: Specializing in specific market segments
  • Integrated Strategies: Balancing multiple approaches
Resource-Based View

Teams developed unique capabilities and resources:

  • R&D Capability: Investment in innovation capacity
  • Brand Strength: Building customer loyalty and recognition
  • Production Capacity: Scaling operations to meet demand
  • Quality Control: Ensuring consistent product quality
Balanced Scorecard

Performance was measured across multiple dimensions:

  • Financial: Profitability, revenue growth, and capital efficiency
  • Market: Market share, customer satisfaction, and brand strength
  • Innovation: R&D effectiveness and new product development
  • Sustainability: Environmental impact and social responsibility
Reflection Questions for Teams
  1. How did your strategic positioning evolve over the course of the simulation?
  2. What trade-offs did you make in resource allocation, and how did they impact your results?
  3. How did you respond to market changes and strategic events?
  4. What would you do differently if you played again?
  5. How do the concepts from this simulation apply to real-world business strategy?
